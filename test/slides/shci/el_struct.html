The Basics

Hartree-Fock (HF)

\[ \ket{\text{HF}} = e^{-\kappa} \ket{0} \]

Optimize the orbitals (\( \kappa \))

Configuration Interaction (CI)

\[ \ket{\text{CI}} = \sum_i C_i\ket{i} \]

Optimize CI coefficients (\( C_i \))

Single reference fails for:

  • Bond-breaking
  • Transition metals
  • Almost all methods in quantum chemistry are based on Hartee-Fock theory in some way. Here we approximate the wave function, psi on the previous slide, as a single configuration. Here a configuration is a particular way of putting electrons in orbitals and an example is shown here at the bottom. For HF wave functions we optimize the orbitals to get the best wave function.
  • I mentioned earlier that the molecules that we want to study are challenging but I didn't say how. It turns out that for these systems and many others, one configuration isn't enough to accurately describe the system. To include more we can express our wave function as a linear combination of configurations. We call this configuration interaction or CI wave function. Here the orbitals are fixed and we optimize the coefficients of the linear expansion. Here's a visual representation of what that expansion might look like for our little toy system.
